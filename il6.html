Why IL-6

The earliest signal in the body's sepsis response.

IL-6 is not just another inflammatory marker — it is one of the first cytokines released in significant quantities when bacterial infection escalates to systemic sepsis. Detecting it at the bedside, before other markers have risen, changes the clinical window for intervention.

Strategic rationale

Focus first. Earn the right to expand.

Starting with a single, well-validated biomarker keeps the assay development scope, validation programme, and clinical claim tight. IL-6 is the strongest possible first target — and the platform is designed to add additional markers as the product matures.

Clinical rationale

IL-6 aligns the clinical, technical, and commercial story.

The choice of IL-6 as the first assay target is deliberate. It is the biomarker most clearly aligned with early sepsis detection, the one with the strongest published clinical validation evidence, and the one that gives Sepsensor a defensible product position that is hard for conventional lab instruments to replicate at the bedside.

Clinical

Rises within hours of infection onset.

IL-6 is produced by macrophages and T-cells within hours of bacterial invasion. It is detectable in plasma before procalcitonin (PCT) peaks and before C-reactive protein (CRP) rises meaningfully — making it a uniquely early warning signal for sepsis risk stratification. Elevated IL-6 at hospital admission is independently associated with increased ICU admission and 28-day mortality.

Technical

A well-characterised immunoassay target.

IL-6 antibody pairs for immunoassays are well-established and commercially available. This reduces the assay development risk significantly and allows the team to focus engineering effort on the detection system and proprietary calibration architecture rather than novel antibody development.

Commercial

Strongest first product wedge.

A single-biomarker product has a crisp, defensible clinical claim: faster IL-6 at the bedside for earlier sepsis decisions. This is easier to validate in a clinical trial, easier to present to hospital procurement, and easier to regulate than a multi-marker panel — while the underlying platform supports expansion.

Published evidence

The IL-6 and sepsis connection is well-established in the literature.

Sepsensor's IL-6-first thesis is grounded in decades of peer-reviewed clinical research. Key findings from landmark studies underpin the clinical rationale for bedside IL-6 measurement.

Foundational study

Hack CE, De Groot ER, Felt-Bersma RJ, et al. "Increased plasma levels of interleukin-6 in sepsis." Blood. 1989;74(6):1704–1710.

One of the first studies to demonstrate that IL-6 is significantly elevated in sepsis patients compared to healthy controls. Established IL-6 as a primary cytokine in the systemic inflammatory response to infection and a candidate diagnostic biomarker.

Diagnostic validation

Harbarth S, Holeckova K, Froidevaux C, et al. "Diagnostic value of procalcitonin, interleukin-6, and interleukin-8 in critically ill patients admitted with suspected sepsis." Am J Respir Crit Care Med. 2001;164(3):396–402.

A key prospective clinical study demonstrating that IL-6 has strong diagnostic value for confirming sepsis in critically ill patients on ICU admission — with IL-6 elevations preceding procalcitonin and CRP in the early phase, supporting its use as an early detection marker.

Comprehensive review

Tanaka T, Narazaki M, Kishimoto T. "IL-6 in inflammation, immunity, and disease." Cold Spring Harb Perspect Biol. 2014;6(10):a016295.

A comprehensive review of IL-6 biology and its role in systemic inflammation. Documents IL-6's central position in the immune response cascade, its elevation in bacterial sepsis, and its clinical relevance as a target for both diagnostics and therapeutic intervention.

Assay approach

One blood sample. Automated. 10-minute result.

The IL-6 assay runs entirely within the disposable microfluidic cartridge. The operator loads a small plasma sample, inserts the cartridge into the reader, and the system handles the full assay automatically — delivering a quantitative IL-6 result in 10 minutes.

Validated detection range

The assay is designed to cover the clinically relevant IL-6 concentration range for sepsis risk stratification — from healthy baseline levels through to the critically elevated concentrations associated with septic shock.

Expandable to a multi-biomarker panel

The platform architecture supports multiple assay channels on a single cartridge. Future iterations will add additional sepsis biomarkers — such as procalcitonin and CRP — enabling a comprehensive bedside inflammatory panel from a single 10-minute run.

Biomarker comparison

Why not CRP or procalcitonin first?

C-reactive protein and procalcitonin are the most commonly measured sepsis biomarkers in current clinical practice — but both have limitations that make them suboptimal for early bedside detection. IL-6 addresses each limitation directly.

vs CRP

IL-6 rises faster and earlier.

CRP is a downstream acute-phase protein that peaks 24–48 hours after infection onset — driven in large part by IL-6 itself. IL-6 is measurably elevated hours before CRP is clinically informative. For early detection and rapid decision support, IL-6 is the superior leading indicator.

vs PCT

Comparable sensitivity, better early kinetics.

Procalcitonin is widely used for sepsis severity stratification and antibiotic stewardship. IL-6 rises faster in the early infection phase, providing earlier clinical decision support in the critical 0–6 hour window when the treatment intervention has the greatest impact on outcomes.

Platform vision

All three. One cartridge. One result.

The Sepsensor platform roadmap includes IL-6, PCT, and CRP as a combined panel — giving clinicians the full picture of inflammatory state from a single bedside run. IL-6 comes first to establish the platform; the panel follows once the core product is validated.
