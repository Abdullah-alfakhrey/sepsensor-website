Published evidence

The IL-6 and sepsis connection is well-established in the literature.

Foundational study

Hack CE, De Groot ER, Felt-Bersma RJ, et al. "Increased plasma levels of interleukin-6 in sepsis." Blood. 1989;74(6):1704–1710.

First demonstration that IL-6 is significantly elevated in sepsis patients — establishing it as a primary cytokine in the systemic inflammatory response.

Diagnostic validation

Harbarth S, Holeckova K, Froidevaux C, et al. "Diagnostic value of procalcitonin, interleukin-6, and interleukin-8 in critically ill patients admitted with suspected sepsis." Am J Respir Crit Care Med. 2001;164(3):396–402.

IL-6 elevations precede procalcitonin and CRP in the early phase — supporting its use as an early detection marker on ICU admission.

Comprehensive review

Tanaka T, Narazaki M, Kishimoto T. "IL-6 in inflammation, immunity, and disease." Cold Spring Harb Perspect Biol. 2014;6(10):a016295.

Documents IL-6's central position in the immune response cascade and its clinical relevance as a diagnostic and therapeutic target.

Biomarker comparison

Why not CRP or procalcitonin first?

vs CRP

IL-6 rises faster and earlier.

CRP peaks 24–48 hours after infection onset — IL-6 is measurably elevated hours before CRP becomes clinically informative.

vs PCT

Comparable sensitivity, better early kinetics.

IL-6 rises faster in the 0–6 hour window when antibiotic intervention has the greatest impact on outcomes.

Platform vision

All three. One cartridge. One result.

IL-6, PCT, and CRP as a combined panel — IL-6 comes first to establish the platform, the panel follows once validated.
