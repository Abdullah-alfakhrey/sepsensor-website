Team structure

Interdisciplinary by design.

Building a self-calibrating optical biosensor requires simultaneous expertise in assay chemistry, optical hardware, embedded systems, and microfluidics. The team structure reflects the product architecture — not a generic startup configuration.

The people

Five people covering every layer of the platform.

The team was assembled specifically around the Sepsensor product — matching each person's core expertise to an essential product component. There are no generalists filling gaps; each team member owns a critical part of the technical and commercial build.

Co-founder · Bio-assay

Yazan Haidar

MEng Biomedical Engineering

Yazan leads the IL-6 assay development — designing the dual-sandwich immunoassay structure, validating antibody surface chemistry, and defining the biological logic of the detection and calibration system. He is the primary driver of assay feasibility and clinical positioning.

Co-founder · Detection system

Robotics & AI Engineering

Abdullah leads the optical detection system — camera integration, filter selection, image processing pipeline, and embedded signal processing algorithms that convert fluorescence data to quantitative IL-6 concentration. Surface chemistry

Tao Xu

Biochemistry & Surface Science

Tao owns the surface chemistry layer — the functionalisation of glass detection substrates, antibody immobilisation protocols, and the biotin-streptavidin linking chemistry that connects the calibration fluorophore to the assay structure. His work is foundational to the self-calibration architecture.

Electronics

Jaswinder Singh

Electronics Engineering

Jaswinder leads the electronics design for the reader — illumination source control, power management, embedded processor integration, and the hardware interfaces between the optical system and the cartridge handling mechanism. His work ensures the reader functions reliably outside a laboratory setting.

Scientific advisor

Prof. Julien Reboud

Professor, University of Glasgow

Prof. Reboud provides scientific guidance and academic leadership for the Sepsensor project at the University of Glasgow. His expertise in microfluidics, point-of-care diagnostics, and biosensor development underpins the technical credibility of the platform — and his institutional support has been critical in securing lab access, programme entry, and academic validation.

Team thesis

Why this team can build this product.

The founders built the prototype over a year with no external investment — iterating on assay chemistry, optical hardware, and electronics in parallel. That execution track record, combined with the institutional backing of the University of Glasgow and STAC, demonstrates that this is not a paper team.

Technical depth

Robotics, AI, biotech, and electronics — all present.

The combination of robotics and AI engineering (Abdullah), biomedical assay expertise (Yazan), and surface chemistry (Tao) is the exact multidisciplinary profile required to build a self-calibrating optical biosensor. Most early-stage medtech teams lack one or more of these layers.

Execution proof

Functional prototype built without investment.

Sepsensor's founders built a functional prototype over 12 months using programme grants and institutional resources. Prior startup founding experience in the team means commercial execution skills are already present — not something to be acquired.

Academic anchor

University of Glasgow institutional backing.

Prof. Reboud's involvement as scientific advisor and the University of Glasgow's support through the Infinity G programme provides Sepsensor with academic credibility, lab infrastructure, and a validated regulatory awareness that most pre-seed medtech teams cannot access.
