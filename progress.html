Programme support

Three funded programmes. One consistent direction.

Every programme Sepsensor has entered has reinforced the same thesis: a self-calibrating bedside IL-6 sensor is technically viable, clinically necessary, and commercially relevant. The support to date reflects external validation of both the team and the product direction.

University of Glasgow

Infinity G Deep Tech Venture Builder

Infinity G is the University of Glasgow's deep-tech venture building programme. Sepsensor was awarded a grant and full incubation support — including lab access, 3D printing and electronics services, business strategy coaching, and structured milestone reviews. The programme provided the first institutional validation of the commercial thesis.

Glasgow City Council

Wilson's Foundry Innovation Grant

Wilson's Foundry is Glasgow City Council's innovation grant programme for early-stage technology ventures. Sepsensor received grant funding to support continued prototype hardware development, component testing, and materials procurement — enabling the team to advance the device design without diluting equity.

Scottish Technology Accelerator

STAC Cohort 6

STAC (the Scottish Technology Accelerator) is Scotland's leading programme for deep-tech scale and commercialisation. Selection for Cohort 6 provides access to funding, legal and IP services, regulatory pathway development, and investor networks — directly aligned with Sepsensor's pre-clinical trial preparation.

Milestones

A year of structured progress, zero external investment.

Sepsensor's milestone progression demonstrates that the team can execute without large funding rounds — and that the technical and commercial story is strong enough to attract institutional programme support on its own.

01
Origin

SensUs international biosensor competition

The project was founded through SensUs — an international student biosensor competition focused on clinically relevant detection challenges. The competition established the IL-6-for-sepsis concept, formed the core team, and produced the first feasibility validation of the optical detection approach.

02
Physician validation

Clinical interviews and feature validation

The team conducted physician interviews and surveys with healthcare professionals to validate the product concept and refine the feature set. Key findings: strong clinician interest in bedside IL-6, with continuous monitoring every 10 minutes identified as the most-valued capability — now a core product feature.

03
Infinity G

University of Glasgow grant and incubation

Awarded the Infinity G Deep Tech Venture Builder grant. This provided lab access at the University of Glasgow, equipment and consumables for prototype development, electronics and 3D printing services for hardware iteration, and business development support including market sizing and commercial strategy.

04
Wilson's Foundry

Glasgow City Council innovation grant

Received Wilson's Foundry grant funding from Glasgow City Council to support continued hardware development. Funds were used for prototype component procurement, cartridge manufacturing trials, and reader housing iterations.

05
First anti-BSA experiments

Assay surface chemistry validated

Initial experiments validated the antibody surface chemistry on glass substrates. Fluorescence signal was confirmed for the detection and calibration sandwich structures, establishing the core assay chemistry proof-of-concept.

06
STAC Cohort 6

Selected for Scotland's leading deep-tech accelerator

Selected for STAC Cohort 6 — the Scottish Technology Accelerator's scale and commercialisation programme. STAC provides access to legal and IP services, regulatory expertise, investor networks, and milestone-based funding to prepare for clinical trials and Series A.

07
Now

Functional prototype actively being validated

A functional prototype is under active testing. IL-6 concentration measurements are being collected across cartridge batches to demonstrate the self-calibration performance and build the IP evidence base. The team is preparing for formal clinical validation and regulatory pre-submission activities.

What's next

Clinical validation, IP protection, and Series A preparation.

The immediate priorities are completing prototype validation, filing IP, and preparing for a clinical trial protocol submission. Sepsensor is actively seeking strategic investors and clinical partners to accelerate this phase.
