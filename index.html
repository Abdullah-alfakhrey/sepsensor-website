Why Sepsensor

Sepsis does not wait for the central lab queue.

The platform is being shaped around a practical constraint: near-patient testing has to be fast, reliable, and calm enough to fit real clinical environments instead of adding another fragile workflow.

01

Near-patient speed

Sepsensor is positioned around bedside turnaround rather than central-lab dependency, with the goal of making biomarker data useful at the moment it is needed.

02

Built-in self-calibration

The core differentiator is not only optical readout, but the calibration architecture designed to reduce variation across cartridges and measurement conditions.

03

Platform direction

The current assay focuses on IL-6, with the wider platform aimed at future biomarker expansion and more intelligent bedside interpretation over time.

Platform overview

A compact optical system with a disposable test path.

Sepsensor combines a fluorescence reader, signal-processing workflow, and microfluidic cartridge architecture. The system is being developed to keep the readout precise without pushing the calibration burden back to the operator.

Camera-driven readout

Fluorescence is read through a camera and optical filtering approach, then converted into a usable concentration estimate through image analysis.

Calibration is part of the assay design

The platform is being developed around a self-calibrating assay structure so reliability can be engineered into the readout rather than treated as an afterthought.

Disposable cartridge workflow

The cartridge contains the assay path while the reader remains reusable, supporting a format better aligned with routine bedside use.

Progress

Built through research, assay iteration, and venture support.

The project has moved from biosensor competition and dissertation work into a startup-oriented prototype effort spanning assay development, hardware iteration, and external programme support.

  1. 2022

    Research foundation

    Early work centered on self-calibrating rapid sepsis detection and the first versions of the portable biosensor concept.

  2. 2024

    Lab and hardware iteration

    Assay experiments, camera checks, casing edits, and cartridge work moved the concept toward a more integrated prototype.

  3. 2025

    Programme and proposal traction

    Infinity G, Wilson’s Foundry, STAC, and Interface-related work sharpened the external positioning around validation and commercialization.

  4. Now

    Prototype in validation

    The immediate direction is clearer product refinement, partner engagement, and a pilot-ready clinical narrative.
